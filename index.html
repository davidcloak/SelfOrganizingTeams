Choosing The Right People

"Effective project leaders focus on people, product, and process-in that order."

Choosing The Right Person

"Getting the right person means finding those with appropriate technical and behavioral skills."

Qualities to look for

Choosing the right person is about finding people with technical prowess and behavioral skills. Obviously someone who has a skillset that aligns with the needs of the project will be helpful, but if they are rude, lazy and smelly they will negatively effect the team as a whole. Conversely, the nicest little marry sue in the world is not going to be much help to an astrophysicist when he is a carpenter by trade. It is the job of a project leader to find people who strike a good balance between these two extremes in order to put together a functioning team.

Why people matter most

Agile project managment is about people. Agile leaders focus on people first. Its great people that build great products. With that said, its easy to see why choosing the right people is key to any project being successful under the agile mentality.

How To Implement

Over the course of speaking with almost 500 leaders for my weekly “Corner Office” series, I’ve asked every one of them, “How do you hire?” Their answers are always insightful because after years of interviewing countless job candidates, they’ve learned the best approaches to help them get right to the core of who a candidate is and how he or she will work with a team. Learn the strategies these chief executives have developed through trial and error to help you go beyond the polished résumés, pre-screened references and scripted answers, to hire more creative and effective members for your team. And if you’re on the other side of the job hunt, you can gain insight on what your interviewer is really looking for in a candidate.
